Healthcare Provider Financial Benchmarking Tool

Overview

This executive dashboard provides a comprehensive comparison of St. Gabriel Medical Center's financial performance against peer hospitals. The tool facilitates strategic decision-making by benchmarking key metrics such as operating margin, revenue cycle efficiency, cost structure, and productivity. Data are drawn from internal systems and verified external sources, enabling leaders to assess areas of strength and opportunity.

Variances between our results and peer benchmarks highlight where operational improvements or investments may be warranted. The dashboard supports monthly and quarterly reviews, board packet preparation, and service line planning, ensuring our leadership remains data-driven and proactive.

Each KPI is hyperlinked for detailed interpretation and recommended actions. Filters allow you to tailor comparisons by region, bed size, ownership, service line, and timeframe, enhancing relevance for various decision-making scenarios.

Action recommendations are prioritized to drive margin enhancement, cost optimization, and improved productivity. The interactive charts and sortable tables make it easy to identify areas of high variance and drill down for targeted interventions.

Please refer to the footnotes for data sources, definitions, and limitations. For further guidance, contact Finance Strategy & Analytics.1

Recommended Actions

Results indicate targeted interventions are needed in revenue cycle and cost structure, while margin and productivity remain competitive. The following priority actions are recommended for the upcoming quarter, with anticipated financial impact and timelines:

  • Accelerate AR follow-up to reduce Days in AR by 6+ days within 90 days.
  • Conduct root cause analysis of denied claims; implement top-3 fixes by month-end.
  • Engage payer relations team to improve payer mix and renegotiate contracts by Q4.
  • Initiate cost containment review in high-cost service lines; report savings opportunities in 60 days.
  • Expand Lean process improvement pilots for discharge workflows.
  • Optimize staffing schedules to match patient volumes, targeting a 5% reduction in overtime costs.
  • Update denial management training for front-line revenue cycle staff this month.
  • Monitor and report progress monthly to Finance Leadership Team and Board.

Executing these actions is expected to improve net margin by 0.8–1.5 points and reduce excess costs by $0.7–1.2M over the next two quarters.

Data Notes & Sources

Benchmark data are sourced from the St. Gabriel internal financial reporting system, Premier Healthcare Database, and Modern Healthcare’s annual survey.1 Peer group selection is filterable above. Metrics definitions and calculation methods are available in the internal KPI handbook.

Limitations: Data reflect the most recent quarter and may lag in peer group updates. Outliers are excluded from median and quartile calculations. For further clarification, contact Finance Strategy & Analytics.

Metric Details & Suggested Actions

Net Operating Margin (%)

Interpretation: Measures overall profitability after operating expenses. Positive variance indicates strong performance, but continued monitoring is needed to sustain gains.

Suggested Actions: Review cost drivers regularly and assess opportunities for revenue enhancement. Consider reinvesting surplus margin in strategic growth areas.

EBITDA Margin (%)

Interpretation: Indicates core operating profitability excluding non-cash expenses. Higher EBITDA supports capital needs and resilience.

Suggested Actions: Strengthen payer mix, manage expenses, and invest in service lines with high EBITDA contribution.

Days in AR

Interpretation: Elevated Days in AR signal slower cash collections and potential revenue leakage. Current results are above target and median.

Suggested Actions: Accelerate AR follow-up, automate billing processes, and engage with payers to resolve outstanding accounts.

Denial Rate (%)

Interpretation: High denial rates reduce net revenue and increase administrative costs. Current rates suggest process gaps and need for staff education.

Suggested Actions: Audit denials, implement targeted staff training, and automate claim correction workflows.

Cost per Adjusted Discharge ($)

Interpretation: Higher costs per discharge may reflect inefficiencies or case mix complexity. Review for outliers and process bottlenecks.

Suggested Actions: Drive Lean process improvements, review supply chain contracts, and benchmark service line costs.

FTEs per Adjusted Occupied Bed

Interpretation: Staffing efficiency is strong relative to peers, but further optimization may yield savings.

Suggested Actions: Continue schedule optimization, cross-train staff, and monitor overtime usage.
